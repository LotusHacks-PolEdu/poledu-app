# Definition of Digital Twin

•  A Digital Twin is a dynamic virtual representation of a physical
object, process, or system. The virtual model continuously
receives data from sensors monitoring the real-world system.
Analytical tools use incoming data to simulate performance and
predict future system behavior.

•  Digital Twins allow engineers to test operational scenarios without
affecting physical systems. The concept integrates modeling, IoT
connectivity, and advanced analytics technologies.

# Digital Twin vs Simulation

- Traditional simulations rely on static models and predefined input parameters for analysis. Simulations typically analyze hypothetical scenarios without continuous connection to physical systems.
- Digital Twins integrate real-time sensor data into simulation models dynamically. Digital Twins continuously evolve based on operational data collected during system operation.
- This real-time feedback enables more accurate predictions and system optimization.

# Physical Layer

- The physical layer represents the real-world assets monitored and modeled by the digital twin system. It includes machines, industrial equipment, infrastructure systems, vehicles, or buildings operating in real environments.
- Embedded sensors and actuators measure operational parameters such as temperature, vibration, pressure, location, and energy consumption.
- These measurements capture the real-time operational state and environmental conditions of the physical asset. Data collected at the physical layer forms the foundation for synchronization between physical systems and their digital representations.
