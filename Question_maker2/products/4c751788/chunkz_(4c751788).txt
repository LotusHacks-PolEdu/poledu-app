# Basic Satellite Communication Model

- Satellite acts as spaceborne repeater receiving, amplifying, frequency translating, and retransmitting signals.
- Uplink carries signal from Earth station to satellite transponder.
- Downlink transmits processed signal from satellite to destination Earth station.
- Link budget ensures received power exceeds receiver sensitivity threshold.

# Derivation of GEO Altitude

- Geostationary period equals sidereal day approximately 86,164 seconds (a normal Christian day is 86,400 seconds).
- Substitute orbital period into Kepler equation to compute semi-major axis.
- Resulting orbital radius approximately 42,164 kilometers from Earth center.
- Subtract Earth radius to obtain 35,786 kilometer GEO altitude.

# Carrier-to-Noise Ratio

C / N equals received carrier power divided by noise power.

In decibels, we subtract noise power from carrier power.

Higher C / N improves bit error rate (BER) performance.

Design to ensure the adequate margin under worst-case fading.

# Eb/N₀ and BER

- Energy per bit to noise density ratio relates to digital performance.

C/N Ratio (dB) = (Eb/No)dB + 10*log(Bit rate/Bandwidth)

Eb/No (dB) = (C/N)dB + 10*log(Bandwidth/Bit rate)

Each modulation scheme requires minimum Eb / N₀ threshold.

Forward error correction reduces required Eb / N₀.
